Question 1

What is the name of the tiny units that all living things are made of?
Answer: Cells

Question 2

True or false: You can see most cells clearly using only your eyes.
Answer: False

Question 3

What tool do scientists use to see cells clearly?
Answer: A microscope

Question 4

Which part of a plant cell is strong and stiff and helps the cell keep its shape?
Answer: The cell wall

Question 5

Which part of the cell controls what goes in and out of the cell?
Answer: The cell membrane

Question 6

Which cell part contains chlorophyll and carries out photosynthesis?
Answer: Chloroplasts

Question 7

Why do chloroplasts look green?
Answer: Because they contain chlorophyll

Question 8

Name the jelly-like substance inside a cell where chemical reactions happen.
Answer: Cytoplasm

Question 9

Which cell part releases energy from food?
Answer: Mitochondria

Question 10

Name one structure that plant cells have but animal cells do not.
Answer: Cell wall
(Chloroplasts or large vacuole are also correct)

Question 11

Why do onion cells not look green under the microscope?
Answer: They do not contain chloroplasts

Question 12

What is the function of red blood cells?
Answer: To carry oxygen around the body

Question 13

Why do red blood cells not have a nucleus?
Answer: To make more space for haemoglobin

Question 14

Which specialised cell carries electrical signals in the body?
Answer: A neurone (nerve cell)

Question 15

What is a group of similar cells working together called?
Answer: A tissue

Question 16

What do we call a structure made of different tissues working together, like the heart or a leaf?
Answer: An organ

Question 17

Which term means a group of organs working together to do the same job?
Answer: An organ system

Bonus Question (optional)

Who first used the word “cells” after looking at cork under a microscope?
Answer: Robert Hooke
